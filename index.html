八九十年代的中国，汽车还是遥远的传说，自行车驮着整座城市上下班。你在二叔的音像铺打工——骑车把翻录好的磁带送到录像厅、发廊和学校门口。客人在路边等着，靠上道才能交货；攒下的工钱，就换橱窗里那些索尼、爱华、松下：省电的、防震的、带重低音的，还有传说中1979年的那台起点之机。路上别撞翻车斗，也别让耳机里的音乐停下来——每台机器的音质、耗电、绞带手感都不一样，便宜机器听得出磁带的沙沙声。

Where car culture had Forza Horizon and its garage of classics, boom-era China had the Walkman. You're the delivery kid for your uncle's tape-dubbing shop: customers wait curbside — swing into the top lane to hand off their order, and your wages fund the collection. SONY, AIWA, Panasonic, Toshiba — each machine has real stats: sound quality (score multiplier), battery thrift, anti-shock (your pencil-rewind rescue window), bass boost, waterproofing. Cheap decks literally sound worse. Ride from 1984 to the year 2000, when the MP3 arrives.
